The figure presents bar charts providing a quantitative comparison of the performance of the three architectures (Residual-Corr, Physics-Reg, Black-Box) in terms of Average RMSE (a) and R² Score (b). The visualizations distinguish between performance on the Training set (solid bars) and the Test set (hatched bars).

(a) Average RMSE (N·m): On the training dataset, the Black-Box model achieves the lowest RMSE (0.0636 N·m), outperforming both Physics-Reg (0.0678 N·m) and Residual-Corr (0.0707 N·m). However, this trend is starkly reversed on the unseen test data. The Residual-Corr architecture achieves the lowest Test RMSE (0.0903 N·m), closely followed by Physics-Reg (0.0923 N·m). In contrast, the Black-Box model performs worst on the test set, yielding the highest Test RMSE of 0.1003 N·m. This quantitative inversion strongly highlights the superior generalization capabilities of the physics-informed models over the purely data-driven Black-Box approach, which clearly suffers from overfitting to the training distribution.

(b) R² Score: A consistent trend is observed in the R² scores, which measure the proportion of variance explained by the models. The Black-Box model exhibits the lowest Test R² score (0.8932). Conversely, the Residual-Corr and Physics-Reg models achieve higher Test R² scores of 0.9106 and 0.9080, respectively. While the Train R² scores are relatively comparable across all models (ranging around 0.86 to 0.87), the test performance explicitly validates that the physics-informed architectures provide more robust and accurate predictions on unseen data.

Conclusion: The quantitative comparison emphatically underscores the benefit of incorporating physical knowledge into the neural network architecture. While the high capacity of the Black-Box model allows it to fit the training data more closely, the inductive biases embedded within the Residual-Corr and Physics-Reg models yield significantly improved generalization. This results in lower test errors and higher test R² scores, making them far more suitable for robust real-world deployment.
